Your Role in One Line
You build the study abroad consultancy's Flutter dashboard — the visual face of Smartilee that judges see,
tap through, and judge the product on. Your job is to show everything working in real time: live student
conversations appearing, handoff alerts firing, customer profiles with country preferences and IELTS scores

SCOPE FOR THIS HACKATHON
The goal is focused — build the AI-powered WhatsApp layer and show it working through a clean dashboard.
Happilee handles the WhatsApp infrastructure. The Flask backend handles the intelligence. Your Flutter app
shows the counsellor what is happening and gives them control. No payment flows, no complex onboarding.
Just the core dashboard working cleanly — with study abroad context throughout (country packages, IELTS
scores, visa status, scholarship interest).

SCREEN BY SCREEN BREAKDOWN

1. DashboardScreen
Four stat cards in a 2x2 grid:
· Total Student Messages Today
· AI Reply Rate (%)
· Enquiry Conversions Today <-- replaces 'Cart Recoveries'
· Churn Risk Students
Below the cards: BarChart (fl_chart) showing message volume by hour for today.
Below chart: PieChart showing student count by category — Champion (amber), Loyal (indigo), At Risk (deepOrange),
Lost (red), New (teal), Bargain Hunter (purple).
Bottom navigation bar: Dashboard / Conversations / Students / Packages / Handoff Queue.
Data fetched on initState and refreshed every 30 seconds with a Timer.
2. ConversationsScreen
ListView of all conversations joined with customer name, ordered by most recent.
Each tile shows: student name bold, last message truncated to 60 chars, relative timestamp, IntentBadge chip, and
country flag emoji (DE/FR/NL) based on preferred_country.
Real-time Supabase subscription on conversations — new rows prepend to top with 1.5s highlight animation.
Tap navigates to ConversationDetailScreen. Empty state view when no conversations.
3. ConversationDetailScreen
WhatsApp-style chat bubbles. Inbound messages are grey bubbles left-aligned. Outbound messages are indigo bubbles
right-aligned.
Under each outbound bubble: small italic grey caption showing intent_label, action_taken, and package_context (e.g.
'package_enquiry · auto_reply · Germany').
Auto-scroll to bottom on load and on new message.
AppBar shows student name, country flag, and an Escalate to Counsellor button — tapping inserts into handoff_queue
and sets is_handoff_active=true.
4. CustomersScreen
Search bar at top. Below it a horizontal row of filter chips — All, Champion, Loyal, At Risk, Lost, New — each in its
category colour.
Second row of filter chips by country: All Countries, Germany, France, Netherlands — filters by preferred_country.
Main content: ListView of CustomerCard widgets showing name, phone, CategoryBadge, ChurnScoreBar,
preferred_country with flag, and last active timestamp.
Tap navigates to CustomerProfileScreen.

5. CustomerProfileScreen
Four sections:
SECTION 1 — Identity Card: name, phone, language, tone preference, CategoryBadge.
SECTION 2 — Study Abroad Profile (NEW — replaces generic purchase info):
· Preferred country with flag · Interested package name
· Education level · Field of study · IELTS score
· Budget range · Churn score bar + risk badge
SECTION 3 — Enquiry History (replaces cart contents):
Active or abandoned enquiry_events for this student. If an active enquiry exists, show country, package_id, status, and
date. 'No active enquiry' empty state otherwise.
SECTION 4 — Conversation History: last 10 conversations as simple tiles with intent + package_context caption.
AppBar: student name + Escalate to Counsellor button.
6. PackagesScreen
NEW SCREEN — shows all 3 study abroad packages from the packages Supabase table.
Three CountryPackageCard widgets, one per country (Germany, France, Netherlands).
Each card shows:
· Country name + flag emoji · Package name
· Tuition fee range · Living cost range
· Duration · Intake months
· Top 3 universities listed · Scholarships available
· Eligible students count (query: COUNT from customers WHERE preferred_country=X)
Data fetched from packages table via get_all_packages() Supabase query.
This screen is the sales tool — counsellors can quickly pull it up during a WhatsApp handoff call.
7. HandoffQueueScreen
All handoff_queue rows where status=pending, ordered by created_at desc, joined with customer name, phone, and
preferred_country.
Each card shows: name bold, phone, country interest, reason, time since escalation.
Green Resolve button — updates status to resolved, resolved_at=now(), sets is_handoff_active=false, animates card
out.
Real-time Supabase subscription on handoff_queue — new pending rows trigger SnackBar alert: 'New escalation:
[name] — [country]'.
8. ChurnRiskScreen
All customers where churn_score > 0.7, ordered by score descending.
Cards show name, phone, CategoryBadge, ChurnScoreBar, preferred_country with flag, days inactive.
Send Re-engagement button — calls Flask /generate-reply with intent='churn_risk' and shows SnackBar:
'Re-engagement sent to [name] about [country]'.
No structural changes from original — only the country flag and preferred_country display are new.
