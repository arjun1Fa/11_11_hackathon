Your Role in One Line
You are the memory of Smartilee. Every student profile, consultation conversation, package enquiry, country
preference, application event, and the entire study abroad knowledge base lives in your Supabase database.
You are the critical path — nothing works until your tables exist. You also own the RAG knowledge base that
powers the AI's ability to answer student questions about packages, visa rules, scholarships, tuition, and
living costs for Germany, France, and the Netherlands.
THE CORE CONCEPT YOU OWN — KNOWLEDGE BASE + PGVECTOR
The study abroad consultancy's entire knowledge — country packages, visa rules, scholarship details, tuition
ranges, living costs, eligibility, intake dates, FAQs — is stored in a knowledge_base table. Each row has a
content column (plain text) and an embedding column (vector). When a student WhatsApps a question like
'What is the tuition fee for Germany?' or 'Can I get a scholarship for France?', the AI engineer's RAG pipeline
converts the question into a vector, finds the most relevant knowledge base rows using cosine similarity
search, and feeds that context to the LLM before generating a reply. The student gets a precise, accurate
answer — not generic internet knowledge.

FAQs — Germany Package
Q: Is education really free in Germany?
A: Public universities charge little to no tuition. Students pay only
a semester contribution (150–350 EUR) covering admin and
transit pass. Living costs are the main expense.
Q: What is a blocked account and how much do
I need?
A: A blocked account proves you can financially support yourself.
You must deposit approximately 11,208 EUR (as of 2024). Our
team guides you through opening it with Deutsche Bank or
Fintiba.
Q: Do I need to know German?
A: Master's programs in English exist widely at TU Munich, RWTH
Aachen, etc. German language (B1/B2) is required for
German-taught programs. We advise based on your chosen
course.
Q: What is APS verification?
A: APS (Academic Evaluation Centre) is mandatory for Indian
students. It verifies your academic certificates. Processing takes
4–6 weeks. We guide you through the entire process.

Q: Can I work part-time in Germany?
A: Yes. International students can work up to 120 full days or 240
half days per year. Minimum wage is 12.41 EUR/hour, which can
cover a significant portion of living costs.

FAQs — France Package
Q: Is GMAT mandatory for French business
schools?
A: Top schools like HEC Paris and ESSEC strongly prefer GMAT
(avg 690+). Some programs offer waivers for candidates with
strong professional experience. We advise you case by case.
Q: What is Campus France?
A: Campus France is the French government agency that
manages study permit applications for most countries. All
students must register and attend an interview before applying for
a visa. We guide the entire process.
Q: Can I work while studying in France?
A: Yes. International students may work up to 964 hours per year
(approx 20 hours/week). French minimum wage is 11.65
EUR/hour. Students in Paris can cover 40–60% of living costs this
way.
Q: What is the Eiffel Excellence Scholarship?
A: A French government scholarship covering tuition, a monthly
stipend (~1,181 EUR), housing allowance, and travel. Very
competitive — we help shortlisted students with the application.
Q: Is French language required?
A: Most top business school programs (HEC, ESSEC, EDHEC)
are fully in English. French language proof is not required for
English-track programs, though basic French helps with daily life.

PGVECTOR SETUP — RUN FIRST IN SUPABASE SQL EDITOR
CREATE EXTENSION IF NOT EXISTS vector; CREATE OR REPLACE FUNCTION match_knowledge_base(
query_embedding vector(1536), match_count int DEFAULT 4, filter_country text DEFAULT NULL,
filter_category text DEFAULT NULL ) RETURNS TABLE ( id uuid, package_id text, country text,
category text, title text, content text, similarity float ) LANGUAGE plpgsql AS $$ BEGIN RETURN
QUERY SELECT kb.id, kb.package_id, kb.country, kb.category, kb.title, kb.content, 1 -
(kb.embedding <=> query_embedding) AS similarity FROM knowledge_base kb WHERE kb.embedding IS
NOT NULL AND (filter_country IS NULL OR kb.country = filter_country) AND (filter_category IS
NULL OR kb.category = filter_category) ORDER BY kb.embedding <=> query_embedding LIMIT
match_count; END; $$;
The updated RPC function adds optional filter_country and filter_category parameters. If a student asks
specifically about Germany, the AI engineer can pass filter_country='Germany' to retrieve only
Germany-relevant knowledge base rows — making RAG results more precise.
